Our Chaplaincy Ministry

What is Being a Chaplain?

A **Chaplain** is a minister or faith leader who provides spiritual care outside the walls of a church. They serve in diverse settings like hospitals, schools, military units, prisons, and corporations.

The role of a Chaplain is to offer **non-judgmental spiritual support, emotional comfort, and guidance** to people of all faiths (or no faith) during times of crisis, change, or spiritual need. They are a presence of peace, compassion, and hope, dedicated to the holistic well-being of individuals in secular or specialized environments.

Our Chaplain Academy: Training for Service

The International St John's Apostolic Faith Mission Church **Chaplain Academy** is dedicated to raising, training, and ordaining qualified Chaplains for service within the church and the broader community. Our Academy provides comprehensive training that integrates theological study with practical, professional skills in counseling, crisis intervention, and spiritual diplomacy.

**What our Academy offers:**

  • In-Depth Theological Study
  • Practical Ministry Skills
  • Official Ordination and Certification
  • Hospital & Trauma Response Training
